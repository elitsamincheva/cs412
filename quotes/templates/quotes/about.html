About Angela Davis

Angela Davis is a revolutionary scholar, activist, and abolitionist. A former member of the Communist Party and affiliated with the Black Panthers, she was placed on the FBI’s Most Wanted List in 1970 and later acquitted. Her work spans philosophy, feminism, and prison abolition, emphasizing intersectionality and systemic change.

Davis critiques carceral feminism, linking the 1994 crime bill to mass incarceration, and advocates for abolishing prisons and reallocating resources toward social justice. She stands for collective liberation, racial and gender justice, and dismantling oppressive systems through radical imagination and activism.

“If one does not engage in the ongoing work when such a moment arises, we cannot take advantage of the opportunities to change.” — Angela Davis

About the Creator

I chose Angela Davis because her work in activism and social justice really resonates with me.
